Caesar Cipher - ROT13

The Caesar Cipher was one of the first forms of 'encryption' and is one of the more well known encryption techniques.
ROT13 is one of the more common Caesar cipher variations and simple takes a letter, counts 13 places after that letter and replaces it with the one it lands on i.e if you put the letter A it will substitute A for N which is 13 places infront of A.

One kinda 'cool' thing about ROT13 is that it decrypts its self as well an encrypts its self. So the code I have to encrypt your word/sentence is not changed, so if you click on the button and click it again it will reverse the cipher.
